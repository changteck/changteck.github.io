BAT Creators: Intro To Running Part 2/3, 23 Feb 2021

Feel motivated to get started on your fitness journey? Running is one of the simplest activities that you can get started on. It doesn't have to be constantly an all-out effort that leaves you on the bed the whole day!

Appended below is a program I call the '1-2-3-4' Program - 1 Program, 2 Kilometers target, 3 times a week, for 4 weeks.


NOTE: It is important to note that this is a general program and you have to tailor it to your personal stress, energy and mental levels of the day itself. There are different fitness zones listed below that you can use as reference, but ultimately, you have to listen to your body!


Terms I will be using:

  • Green (Easy): Panting, but able to sing your national anthem in one breath.
  • Yellow (Medium): Speaking one sentence before needing to breathe
  • Red (Hard): Short, broken words in between breaths

    • Please take note that it is entirely OK to walk and take a breath in the Green Zone! As long as you keep moving, that counts as mileage too!

  • Week 1: 2x 3 mins run in Green, 1x 2 mins run in Yellow
  • Week 2: 2x 4 mins run in Green, 1x 3 mins run in Yellow
  • Week 3: 2x 5 mins run in Green, 1x 4 mins run in Yellow
  • Week 4: 2x 6 mins run in Green, 1x 5 mins run in Yellow

    • Still find the program difficult? Reduce everything by 1 - 2 minutes! It doesn't strictly have to follow what I have proposed and everything hugely depends on your personal comfort, fitness and energy levels to stay fit. Can run only 2 minutes everyday? That's better than someone still sitting on the couch wondering where time has gone to! That's it from me this week, and I hope to create more content like this that would motivate people to pick up running as part of or to start their fitness regimes! I'm open to any form of constructive feedback and as usual, #tothemoon for all BAT HODLers!

    Some of you hate running, some of you have a sedentary lifestyle or some don't even know what is fitness, so I hope that this would be something you can take reference to know that running doesn't have to be an all-out effort that makes you feel like dying after every session.


  • First & foremost, we need to understand the main reason why you want to run, or even get started on fitness? This is the most important driver in your fitness journey when the going gets tough
  • Next, what are your main aims after addressing your 'why?'. Are you looking to keep a general level of fitness, or are you looking to complete a full-marathon this year?
  • The most crucial thought process you have to go through would be to understand how much time you have in a day to be able to commit yourself to fitness?

    • If you are able to answer all 3 questions above without a hitch, you are good to go! Stay tuned for next week's content where I share a simple program to get you started.
